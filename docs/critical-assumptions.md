# Critical Assumptions

## Instruction
Identify assumptions that could cause your final prototype to fail if they are wrong.

| Assumption ID | Category | Assumption | Related Requirement/User Story | Risk Level | Current Evidence | How to Test |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A-01 | User Problem | Students want a single searchable platform instead of scattered Facebook groups. | FR-06 / US-03 | High | Lab 03: 116 of 120 used 3+ sources | Ask testers to find a room and observe if they prefer the platform |
| A-02 | Value Proposition | Direct owner contact (no agents) is valuable enough to attract students. | FR-02 / US-05 | High | Lab 03: agent cost was a common pain | Prototype test measuring interest in direct contact |
| A-03 | Usability | Students can search and filter listings without help. | FR-06 / US-03 | High | No evidence yet | Task-based prototype test (find a room under budget) |
| A-04 | Usability | Users understand the availability status labels (Available/Limited/Unavailable). | FR-08 / US-06 | Medium | No evidence yet | Ask testers what each status means |
| A-05 | Usability | The map helps students judge distance to campus. | FR-07 / US-04 | Medium | Lab 03: unclear distance was top pain | Observe if testers use the map to decide |
| A-06 | Technical Feasibility | Listings load quickly from the backend during real use. | FR-04 / FR-14 | Medium | Prototype runs locally | Measure load time during the test |
| A-07 | Business Logic | Apartment owners would post rooms directly. | FR-03 / US-02 | High | Lab 03: only 3 owners (weak evidence) | Interview more owners; simulate owner posting |
| A-08 | Data Handling | Students trust the listing information enough to contact owners. | FR-15 | Medium | No evidence yet | Feedback question on trust after the test |

## Categories
Use these categories:
- User problem
- Value proposition
- Usability
- Technical feasibility
- Business logic
- Data handling
