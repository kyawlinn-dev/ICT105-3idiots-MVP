# Customer Discovery Summary

## Project Title

Student Apartment Finder

## Target Users Studied

First-year and international students who recently searched for housing, senior students who recently moved, and a few apartment owners who rent to students.

## Number of Responses / Interviews

123 simulated responses (120 students + 3 owners).

## Main Repeated Problem

Finding an available apartment near campus is slow and confusing because listings are scattered across many sources with unclear prices and distances.

## Main Current Workarounds

Facebook student-housing groups (most common), property agents, asking seniors/friends, and walking around to spot "for rent" signs.

## Strongest Evidence Found

112 of 120 students rated the search 4-5 in difficulty and 116 of 120 used three or more sources, with an average search time of about 2.9 weeks. This is strong, repeated evidence that fragmentation is a real pain, not just our opinion.

## Weak Evidence or Unclear Findings

Owner interest is based on only 3 responses, so it is weak. Although 100 of 120 students said they would try a better solution, this is stated intent, not proven adoption.

## What We Must Revise

Gather more evidence from apartment owners, and test real adoption behavior (not just stated willingness) before assuming students will switch platforms.

## Decision for Next Lab

Choose one:
- **Continue with same problem direction** (focus the first segment on new and international students)
- Narrow the target user segment
- Revise the problem statement
- Change the project direction

## Updated Problem Statement

New and international students near the university spend about 2.9 weeks searching scattered, unreliable sources to find an affordable room close to campus, and have no easy way to find a roommate — so they waste time, overpay, and feel stressed during their first weeks.
