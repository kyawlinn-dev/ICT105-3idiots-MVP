# Lab 04 - User Persona

## 1. Persona Name
Mia - Year 1 International Student

## 2. User Type
A new international student searching for off-campus housing near the university, who is unfamiliar with the area and the local rental market.

## 3. Background and Context
- **Where the problem happens:** When she first arrives for the semester and needs a room close to campus.
- **When it happens:** In her first few weeks, under time pressure before classes start.
- **Digital tools she already uses:** Facebook groups, Google Maps, LINE, and messaging apps — but no single place to search for student housing.

## 4. Goals
- Find an affordable room close to campus quickly and with confidence.
- Understand the real price and distance before contacting anyone.
- Find a compatible roommate to share the rent.
- Avoid agent fees and dealing with middlemen.

## 5. Pain Points from Lab 03 Evidence
| Pain Point | Evidence ID / Respondent | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| Listings scattered across many sources | 116 of 120 students used 3+ sources | She must check several Facebook groups and still misses places |
| Unclear price / distance to campus | 27 of 120 (top pain) | She cannot tell how far or how expensive a room really is |
| Listings already taken / out of date | 25 of 120 | She contacts owners about rooms that are already gone |
| No easy way to find a roommate | 71 of 120 sought a roommate | She finds a place but no one to share the cost |

## 6. Current Alternatives / Workarounds
| Current Alternative | Weakness / Limitation | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Facebook student-housing groups | Posts get buried; no search or filter | Survey (most common source) |
| Property agents | Fees and extra steps; slow replies | Interview notes |
| Asking seniors / friends | Limited, luck-based, few options | Survey |

## 7. Design Implications
- **The system should support:** searching and filtering listings in one place, clear price and distance on every listing, direct contact with owners, and a roommate-request board.
- **The team should avoid:** agent involvement, payment processing, and complex features that don't serve the first search.
- **Most important MVP need:** a single searchable place to find nearby rooms with clear price and distance, and contact the owner directly.
